Miscellaneous

Here is a list of all the activities I do outside of classwork and research.

Volunteering

National Science Bowl

I participated in middle and high school Science Bowl from 7th through 12th grade. Starting from high school, I coached the Harker Middle School team and volunteered at the SLAC regional science bowl. During undergrad, I continued to participate in Science Bowl as a volunteer. In addition to volunteering as a moderator at the Northeast Regionals and the MIT Invitational Science Bowl, I wrote questions for the MIT Invitational and have advised the Joaquin Miller Middle School team. This year (2023), I had the privilege of attending the National Science Bowl finals in person as a volunteer.

HKN

I am an inductee for the Eta Kappa Nu National Honor Society, an organization based on academic performance and volunteer work. As part of my work for HKN, I compiled student evaluations on well-known computer science courses, including Math for Computer Science and Graduate Machine Learning, noting the parts of the course that were the most helpful, areas that students tended to find difficult, and knowledge that students felt would be useful before taking the course.

Teaching

Introduction to Machine Learning (TA)

I helped teach the introductory undergraduate machine learning course at MIT for two semesters as a lab assistant (LA) and two semesters as a teaching assistant (TA). My duties included leading hands-on lab sessions, holding weekly office hours, monitoring class forums, collaborating with professors on the delivery of course content, and playtesting/grading written exams.

MIT HSSP

This summer, I will be teaching a six week long course on Randomized Algorithms as part of MIT's HSSP program for middle and high schoolers.
